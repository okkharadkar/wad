About the City

Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar, formerly Aurangabad, is a historic city in Maharashtra known for its majestic forts, intricate Mughal architecture, and cultural legacy. It is the gateway to the UNESCO World Heritage Sites – Ajanta and Ellora Caves.

The city is famous for its hand-woven Himroo shawls, bustling markets, and a blend of ancient and modern lifestyles. A perfect place for history enthusiasts and curious travelers alike.

Highlights

Ajanta & Ellora Caves

Marvel at ancient rock-cut Buddhist and Hindu caves with intricate carvings and paintings.

Historic Gates

The city once had 52 gates, many of which still stand as symbols of its fortified legacy.

Cultural Blend

Experience a mix of Maratha, Mughal, and modern Maharashtrian traditions in food, music, and art.
